📄 LMI Program Guide
Community Reinvestment Act — What it means for your buyers

What is LMI? Low-to-Moderate Income (LMI) refers to census tracts where the median family income is at or below 80% of the Area Median Income (AMI). Under the Community Reinvestment Act (CRA), lenders are encouraged to make mortgage loans in these areas.

Why does it matter for your buyers? LMI zone properties may qualify for special mortgage programs with reduced down payments, lower rates, and relaxed qualification standards. Your MLO partner specializes in these programs.

Income categories:

  • Low Income — Tract MFI < 50% of AMI
  • Moderate Income — Tract MFI 50–79% of AMI
  • Middle Income — Tract MFI 80–119% of AMI
  • Upper Income — Tract MFI ≥ 120% of AMI

How to use this tool: Use the LMI Search tab to look up any ZIP code before showing properties. Identifying LMI zones early helps match buyers to the best programs.

Questions? Contact your MLO partner directly using the business card on the Home tab.

💡 Tips for Realtors
  • Save your LMI searches so your MLO partner can match buyers to programs
  • Use "Request Pre-Qualification" for buyers ready to move forward — you'll get a response quickly
  • Share open house locations so your MLO can prepare LMI materials in advance
  • LMI buyers often qualify for down payment assistance — always check before ruling out a buyer
